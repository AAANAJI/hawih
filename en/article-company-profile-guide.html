How to Write a Professional Company Profile

Hawih studio · Riyadh · June 2026

What makes a profile persuasive — content before design.

In this article

  1. Start with the essential sections
  2. Write for the reader, not about yourself
  3. Let design serve the content

Start with the essential sections

A strong profile answers the client's questions in a logical order: who we are, what we offer, why us, our work, and how to reach us. Start from this skeleton, then tailor it to your business.

Write for the reader, not about yourself

Shift the focus from “we provide” to “you get.” Talk about the benefit the client gains, and back it with real numbers and examples instead of generic phrases.

Let design serve the content

Good design makes reading easier, not busier: a clear hierarchy, comfortable spacing, and infographics for key numbers. A bilingual version reaches a wider audience.

Frequently asked questions

How many pages should a profile be?

There's no fixed number; what matters is covering what the reader needs without padding. Clarity beats length.

Should I write it myself or hire a specialist?

If you're clear on your message you can start; a content specialist then refines and structures it to be more persuasive.

Do I need Arabic and English versions?

If your audience or partners are bilingual, yes — and that's common in the Saudi market.
